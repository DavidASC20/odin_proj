Tteok-Bokki Recipe

Description

Tteok-bokki is a popular Korean street food dish made from cylindrical rice cakes, fish cakes, and vegetables, all cooked in a spicy and sweet gochujang-based sauce. It's known for its chewy texture and bold flavors, offering a satisfying mix of sweetness, heat, and umami. Often enjoyed as a quick snack or casual meal, tteok-bokki is a staple in Korean cuisine.

Ingredients

  • Rice Cakes (Garaetteok) - 2 cups, cylindrical shape
  • Fish Cakes (Eomuk) - 1 cup, sliced
  • Gochugaru (Korean Red Chili Flakes) - 1-2 tablespoons
  • Gochujang (Korean Red Chili Paste) - 2-3 tablespoons
  • Soy Sauce - 1 tablespoon
  • Sugar - 1-2 tablespoons
  • Garlic - 1-2 cloves, minced
  • Water or Anchovy Broth - 2-3 cups
  • Vegetables (Cabbage, Carrots, Onion) - 1 cup, optional
  • Green Onions - 2, sliced
  • Hard-boiled Eggs - 2, optional
  • Sesame Seeds - for garnish, optional

Steps

  1. Preparation: Slice fish cakes, chop vegetables, and mince garlic. Soak rice cakes in warm water if they are hard.
  2. Broth: In a large pan, add 2-3 cups of water or anchovy broth and bring to a simmer.
  3. Sauce Base: Add gochujang, gochugaru, soy sauce, sugar, and minced garlic to the simmering broth. Stir until well-combined.
  4. Rice Cakes: Drain the soaked rice cakes and add them to the pan. Stir gently to ensure they don't stick together.
  5. Simmer: Let the rice cakes cook in the sauce for 7-10 minutes, or until they become soft.
  6. Fish Cakes and Veggies: Add sliced fish cakes and optional vegetables like cabbage, carrots, and onions. Stir and cook for another 5 minutes.
  7. Check Consistency: If the sauce becomes too thick, add a bit more water. If too thin, let it simmer a bit longer.
  8. Taste Test: Adjust seasoning if necessary. Add more sugar for sweetness or more gochujang for heat.
  9. Green Onions: Stir in sliced green onions and cook for another minute.
  10. Garnish: Optionally, top with hard-boiled eggs and sesame seeds.
  11. Serve: Dish out the Tteok-bokki while hot, preferably on a plate to share.
