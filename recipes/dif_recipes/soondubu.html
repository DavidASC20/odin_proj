Soondubu Recipe

Description

Soondubu is a Korean soft tofu stew, often served boiling hot in a stone pot. It typically contains ingredients like soft tofu, vegetables, proteins like beef, pork, or seafood, and is flavored with gochugaru (Korean red chili flakes) and other seasonings. The dish is known for its spicy, savory, and comforting qualities.

Ingredients

  • Soft Tofu - 1 block
  • Protein (Beef, Pork, Seafood) - 1/2 cup
  • Vegetables (Zucchini, Onion, Mushrooms) - 1 cup
  • Garlic - 2-3 cloves, minced
  • Gochugaru (Korean Red Chili Flakes) - 1-2 tablespoons
  • Gochujang (Korean Red Chili Paste) - 1 tablespoon (optional)
  • Sesame Oil - 1 teaspoon
  • Soy Sauce - 1-2 tablespoons
  • Anchovy or Beef Broth - 2 cups
  • Green Onions - 2, chopped
  • Egg - 1 (optional)
  • Salt and Pepper - to taste

Steps

  1. Preparation: Chop vegetables, proteins, and mince the garlic.
  2. Broth: In a pot, bring 2 cups of anchovy or beef broth to a simmer.
  3. Base Flavor: In a separate stone pot or heavy-bottomed pot, heat a teaspoon of sesame oil. Add minced garlic and cook until fragrant.
  4. Protein: Add your choice of protein (beef, pork, seafood) to the pot and cook until browned.
  5. Spices: Add gochugaru and optionally gochujang. Stir to combine.
  6. Vegetables: Add vegetables like zucchini, onion, and mushrooms. Sauté for a couple of minutes.
  7. Combine: Pour the simmering broth into the stone pot with the sautéed ingredients.
  8. Boil: Bring everything to a boil.
  9. Tofu: Break the soft tofu into chunks and add it to the boiling stew.
  10. Season: Add soy sauce, salt, and pepper to taste.
  11. Simmer: Lower the heat and let it simmer for 5-10 minutes.
  12. Egg: Optionally, crack an egg into the stew.
  13. Garnish: Top with chopped green onions.
  14. Serve: Serve boiling hot, directly in the stone pot if possible.
